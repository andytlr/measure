The iPhone 4 has a resolution of 640x960px, The HTC Desire has a resolution of 480x800px. But they both report themselves as being 320px wide.

This is because of pixel density. The iPhone 4 has a pixel density of 2, which means it is twice the pixel size as it reports.

Finding out this info can be hard, manufacturers tout the resolutions of their devices, but they don’t mention pixel density or what size they actually report themselves at. The reality is that both these phones think they’re 320px wide — the Desire has a pixel density of 1.5.

Just open this site on a device you want to test and read what it says. If the device you’re testing has a high pixel density, the grid will probably look a bit pixelated, but should still give an accurate reading.
